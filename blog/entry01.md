# Entry 1
##### 11/3/25-11/9/25

#### Content/Context
In my final year of high school, I am in **APCSA**, where I will be making an educational simulation in nature with my partner for our Freedom Project. Both of us decided that we'll use the same tool to make the simulation called *Godot*, and it's a game engine that allows us to make games that are 2d and 3d that are interactive. In our previous collaboration, we created a game where the user collects fruits. This year though, we decided to make something more education, where our goal is to teach the users playing about the environment, maybe even human impacts. And we'll be animating, scripting, etc on Godot to make it happen.

#### Tool
As I wrote before, the tool I'm using is *Godot*. And I'm using Godot because it's a free and open-source game engine that allows the user to create both 2d and 3d games. Both of us were interested in Godot because of the possibilities of games that other people made. A game I saw someone created on the website was a forest walk, where the user was walking through a realistic forest. And that's cool and I want to add animals and ways people can learn more about them. I've been searching a bit about the tool, searching on youtube some tutorials. And something that caught my eye was this year's showcase of games from Godot that shows a wide variety of games. I saw shooter games, chill life sim, quick paced fighting games, etc. I haven't tinkered with Godot yet because I needed a different laptop to run Godot better, I already filled out a form and I just need to pick it up. I watched a tutorial where the person was demonstrating how to make a simple game from scratch, and he made it in 2d but says that the code used still mostly applies to 3d.

#### Goal
Our goal, as said before, is to make an environmental simulation. We decided to make an educational game that can be fun to the user, so we talked about doing something with nature and expanded that idea to making a simulation. We want many functions in our game, with the ability to see and get discriptions of the flora and fauna that they encounter. And we also want the users to have an impact on the simulation, where pollution happens and that can stack up if not addressed. And we believe that this is a good way to teach the players about taking care of their environment through an interactive way. 

I'll have to talk to my partner, but I wonder if we'll have the user be in the simulation, or be an outside force afftecting the simulation.

#### EDP
For the Engineering Design Process, we both are both between step 2-3, researching more about *Godot*. We are both expanding our knowledge, finding tutorials, and we are going to tinker in the future. I learned that Godot uses GDScript, that shouldn't be too hard to use, and something interesting is in the tutorial I was watching, how audio was added to an object interested me. I think it's the collision for the object, where if the user touches the area around the object, the sound will be played. I already brainstormed a few ideas, and I'll discuss more about it with my partner, for future ideas for our project. And when I get my new device, I'll download and tinker with Godot. 

#### Skills
Consideration is a skill that I'm using right now for our project idea. I know that my project will have an impact on the players and I hope that they'll respond positively to it. Because a goal of the simulation is to teach the players about the environment and how people are impacting it. I want them to think of helping change the real world, to help project and maintain natural spaces around them, and helping animals. Personally, I can't think of any negative impacts that the simulation will cause besides denial of what's happening or being scared of what happening. Because if we head towards human impacts, some people might not want to see the truth, or don't accept the truth of our impacts on nature or the environment around us.
